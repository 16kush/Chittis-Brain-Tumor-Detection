Introduction

Brain tumors are abnormal growths of cells in the brain. They can be noncancerous (benign) or cancerous (malignant). Brain tumors can occur in both children and adults and may vary in their location, size, and impact on brain function.

Types of Brain Tumors

Brain tumors can be classified based on the type of cells they originate from. Some common types of brain tumors include:

  • Acoustic neuroma
  • Astrocytoma
  • Brain metastases
  • Choroid plexus carcinoma
  • Craniopharyngioma
  • Embryonal tumors
  • Ependymoma
  • Glioblastoma
  • Glioma
  • Medulloblastoma
  • Meningioma
  • Oligodendroglioma
  • Pediatric brain tumors
  • Pineoblastoma
  • Pituitary tumors

Symptoms

The symptoms of brain tumors can vary depending on their location and size. Some common symptoms include:

  • Headaches
  • Seizures or convulsions
  • Difficulty thinking, speaking, or finding words
  • Personality or behavior changes
  • Weakness, numbness, or paralysis in one part or one side of the body
  • Loss of balance, dizziness, or unsteadiness
  • Loss of hearing
  • Vision changes

Treatment

The treatment of brain tumors depends on various factors, including the type, size, and location of the tumor. Treatment options may include surgery, radiation therapy, chemotherapy, and targeted therapies. The goal is to remove or control the tumor while preserving brain function and improving the patient's quality of life.
